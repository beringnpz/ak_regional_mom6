5  ESR 2025: Analysis

Note

This is not an Ecosystem Status Report (ESR). This is simply an analysis of regional model output intended to inform Alaska ESRs.

5.1 Summary for ESRs: simulated temperature

5.1.1 Bering Sea

Winter 2025 conditions in the Bering Sea began warmer than average, with the sea ice edge further north than average and warm anomalies in both surface and bottom temperature across the majority of the eastern Bering Sea shelf. Through March and into April, the leading edge of sea ice extended further south, but open water areas appeared in northwestern regions (southwest of St. Lawrence and in the Gulf of Anadyr). The southern portions of the shelf continued to experience warm anomalies in surface and bottom temperature, but the NBS region was cooler than average. Beginning in late May, cold bottom temperature anomalies appeared in the inner shelf in the vicinity of Nunivak Island, while warm bottom temperature anomalies persisted across the rest of the shelf into late August. In contrast, surface temperatures in the early June through July were cooler than average. This cool surface anomaly persisted across most of the shelf (and extended into the basin) through late July, at which point the southern portions shifted to a warm anomaly while the north remained cool. Cluster analysis applied to mean June through August surface and bottom temperature suggests a few other years with this warm bottom, cool surface pattern, including 2023, 1996, and 1998 (Figure 5.4).

5.1.2 Aleutian Islands

Aleutian Island bottom temperatures have low variability over the simulation period. 2025 conditions were near the upper end of the observed range, with anomalies of ~0.3°C in the winter and increasing to 0.4°C above the climatological (1993 – 2022) mean by mid summer. Surface temperatures were also above average for most of the winter and early spring, but approached mean conditions in spring. Stronger warm anomalies reappeared in late July through August, extending across the Aleutian Islands, western Gulf of Alaska, and southern Bering Sea. Cluster analysis suggested these conditions were similar to 2007, 2021, and 2023 (Figure 5.6).

5.1.3 Gulf of Alaska

Like the AI region, Gulf of Alaska bottom temperatures were in the upper 10-20th percentile for most of the year, approximately 0.5 – 0.6°C above the climatological mean. Surface temperatures began the year slightly above average, though there were some cool anomalies inshore in the eastern Gulf region. In June and July, the cooler anomalies expanded their footprint, with the far western regions also cooling. However, in late July through earlier Aug, temperatures rose above mean both inshore and off the shelf, with a 1.36°C anomaly (spatially averaged over the survey region) on Aug 1. This pattern of warm bottom temperatures with warm western, cool eastern surface temperature clusters most closely with 2017, followed by 2001 (Figure 5.8).

ttarget = datetime(2025,1:8,1);
tidx = interp1(t, 1:length(t), ttarget, 'nearest', 'extrap');
nt = length(ttarget);

plt = {Hc.siconc Hc.tob Hc.tos An.siconc An.tob An.tos};
lbl = ["Sea ice", "Bottom temp. (\circC)", "SST (\circC)", ...
       "Anomaly: Sea ice", "Anomaly: Bottom temp. (\circC)", "Anomaly: SST (\circC)"];
np = length(plt);

padend = @(x) [x nan(size(x,1),1); nan(1,size(x,2)+1)];

h = plotgrid('size', [nt np], 'sp', 0, 'mar', 0, 'mt', 0.08);
h.fig.Position(3:4) = [800 600]*1.5;
h.fig.Color = 'w';
for ii = 1:nt
    for jj = 1:np
        axes(h.ax(ii,jj));
        h.bx = A.addboxmap();
        pcolorpad(A.xc, A.yc, plt{jj}(:,:,tidx(ii)));
        shading flat;
        set([h.bx.lblmer; h.bx.lblpar], 'visible', 'off');
    end
end

set(h.ax(:,1), 'colormap', cmocean('ice'), 'clim', [0 1]);
set(h.ax(:,2:3), 'colormap', cmocean('thermal'), 'clim', [-2 15]);
set(h.ax(:,4), 'colormap', cmocean('balance'), 'clim', [-1 1]);
set(h.ax(:,5:6), 'colormap', cmocean('balance'), 'clim', [-3 3]);

for ip = 1:np
    h.cb(ip) = colorbar(h.ax(1,ip), 'north');
    h.cb(ip).Position(2) = 0.93;
    xlabel(h.cb(ip), lbl{ip});
end
set(h.cb, 'axisloc', 'out', 'tickdir', 'out');

labelaxes(h.ax(:,1), string(ttarget), 'northwest');

fname = fullfile(esrfol, 'val_anom_2025');
if ~exist([fname '.png'], 'file')
    export_fig(fname, h.fig, '-png', '-r150', '-nocrop');
end
